SPECIAL PURPOSE TANKS (OTHER LIQUID STORAGE TANKS)

Concrete precast tanks are commonly used for storing liquids other than water in various industrial, commercial, and agricultural applications. These tanks are typically constructed using precast concrete panels that are mostly assembled on-site. Here are some examples of how precast concrete tanks can be used for storing different liquids:

Water Tanks are generally used to store water for various purposes Common types of water tank based on their usage are

ETP Tanks:

ETP tanks are essential for Effluent Treatment Plants, facilitating the treatment of industrial wastewater to meet environmental standards, with various types including Equalization, Primary, Secondary, and Tertiary Treatment Tanks tailored to client specifications.

Chemical Storage Tanks:

Used for storing wide range of chemicals required in industrial processes. Since the concrete materials is resistant to corrosion and can provide stable and durable containment for hazardous chemicals.

Wastewater Treatment Tanks:

Used in sewage treatment plants or industrial facilities to store and process wastewater before it undergoes for treatment or disposal.

Septic Tanks:

Used to separate solids from wastewater, allowing the liquid to undergo further treatment.

Fertilizer Storage Tanks:

Used in agricultural and industrial settings where Biogas is produced and stored through anaerobic digestion.

Biogas Storage Tanks:

Used to store fertilizers since the concrete precast tanks are made as corrosive resistant.

Oil Storage Tanks:

Used to store acids in industrial facilities.

Acid Storage Tanks:

Used for storing various types of oils, including industrial oils and lubricants.

The specific requirements of the stored substances are considered and relevant safety and environmental regulations are complied with when designing the precast concrete tanks for storing liquids other than water. The design and construction of these tanks are tailored to the properties of the liquids to ensure containment and prevent any potential hazards, adhering to industry standards.
