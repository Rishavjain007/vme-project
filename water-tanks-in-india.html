MONOLITHIC TANKS

The term "monolithic" refers to the fact that the tank is constructed as a single, seamless unit, rather than being assembled from multiple components. Monolithic precast water tanks are large, single-piece concrete structures that are cast and cured as a single unit.

Designed to store water for various purposes, such as domestic use, irrigation, firefighting, or industrial processes.

Cast in a controlled environment, in our precast concrete manufacturing facility.

The entire tank, including walls and base, is poured and cured as a single unit, resulting in a seamless structure.

Can be designed in various shapes and sizes to meet specific capacity requirements and site constraints.

Common shapes include rectangular and square configurations.

Transported to the site as a single unit, making the installation process relatively faster compared to on-site construction.

Usually placed on a prepared foundation, and connections for water inflow and outflow are established.

The design reduces the risk of leakage or structural weaknesses that may occur at joints.

To enhance the tank's performance, waterproofing and protective coatings are applied to prevent water penetration and protect the concrete from deterioration over time.

Customized with additional features such as access hatches, inlet/outlet fittings, and overflow systems based on the specific requirements of the project.

Suitable for a variety of applications and are commonly used in areas where large-volume water storage is required. They offer advantages such as strength, durability, and ease of installation, making them a reliable solution for water storage needs.

Tested for water leakage before dispatching for the erection..
